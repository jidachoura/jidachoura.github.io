Syrian Memorial

The saying (Sirna Frata)(please write this in Arabic) was the starting point of this project, which literally means “We have become change”. Because of the current situation in Syria now, the currency has dropped so the saying was used for its dual meaning as a metaphor representing how we have become change. The aim of this project is represent all Syrian martyrs on all sides of the war. Each coin represents a martyr that has died. Moving forward with the saying, Jida decided to engrave the map of the different districts in Syria on coins. Each coin has kept the side that says The Syrian Arab Republic to emphasize and show that although the people are divided they are all united by being Syrian. The other side of the coin has a piece of the map of the city. The amount of coins for each district depends on the number of martyrs each district has lost.
This is still an ongoing project that aims to represent all 14 districts, the pile representing the martyrs in Damascus suburbs was exhibited in London College Of Communication/ University Of The Arts London in the summer 2015 show.
